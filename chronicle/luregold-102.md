# The Annual Review of Year 102
## *Being the Third Ledger of Luregold, Kept by Atir Dumatturel, Bookkeeper*

---

*Year 102 of the Age of Myth. The ink is fresh. The wine is not.*

---

## On the Matter of New Arrivals — Waves Seven Through Ten

The fortress swells. Where once we were seven fools with a pick and a prayer, we are now eighty-eight settlers — eighty-eight living, breathing, eating, drinking, complaining dwarves crammed into corridors that were designed for maybe forty.

Waves seven through ten arrived across the year, adding to the seventy-odd who had trickled in through Year 101. Among them, several worth the ink:

**Rovod Cobaltfortunes** arrived with Wave Eight and has not stopped working since. One hundred and forty-one masterpieces in less than a year. I want to write that again so the next Bookkeeper understands this is not a scribal error: *one hundred and forty-one.* The woman walked through the gate, sat down at a workshop, and apparently decided that sleeping, eating, and socializing were suggestions rather than requirements. I haven't seen her eat. I assume she eats. The ledger suggests she eats. But I've never actually witnessed it.

The others came in the usual fashion — families with children too young for the frontier, old dwarves with nowhere better to go, crafters seeking opportunity, laborers seeking safety. Young **Nish Bootfancies** arrived at three years old. *Three.* I don't know who sends a three-year-old to a frontier fortress during a world war, but she's here now, and she's ours.

Eighty-eight settlers. Zero deaths. Adding to the three artifacts forged last year, we have acquired two more — but I'll come to those presently. The ledger has never been cleaner, and that worries me more than a messy one would.

## The Fourth Artifact — Brandbeguiles

*Summer, Year 102. Mood: Possessed.*

The possession took **Eshtan Roomclutch** in summer. She barred the workshop door — they always bar the door — and emerged holding an **olive wood blowgun** adorned with hanging rings of olive wood and menacing with spikes of water buffalo leather.

*Brandbeguiles. Zesenir.*

A blowgun. A weapon of stealth and subtlety, menacing with spikes of *leather*. I don't know what you shoot out of an artifact blowgun. I don't know who would dare try. It sits in the stockpile and the other dwarves give it a wide berth.

I have noted in the ledger: "Do not let the children play with Zesenir."

Eshtan didn't remember making it, same as the others. The spirits take them, use their hands, and leave them blinking in the workshop with something impossible on the table and no explanation. Four possessions now, in two years. The deep places are *speaking* through our people, and I do not care for what they have to say.

## The Fifth Artifact — Mindfilled

*Year 102. Mood: Secretive.*

**Fath Tinwill** is the only one who wasn't possessed. He entered a *secretive* mood — meaning he was aware, conscious, and *chose* to create what he created. The others were puppets. Fath was a collaborator.

He produced a **lignite throne** encrusted with tapered baguette-cut sapphires, encircled with bands of date palm wood and rectangular sard cabochons, adorned with hanging rings of lychee wood, and menacing with spikes of lignite and sapphire. On the throne is an image of a **giant loon** in giant brown recluse spider silk.

*Mindfilled. Ottanesmul.*

I need to discuss the giant loon.

Nobody in Luregold has ever seen a giant loon. Nobody in Luregold has ever seen brown recluse spider silk. Fath Tinwill, in his secretive state, acquired materials that *should not be here* and assembled them into a throne that depicts a creature he has never encountered, using silk from a spider that doesn't live in our biome.

I asked Fath about it. He looked at me with the haunted expression of a man who remembers exactly what happened in that workshop and said nothing. He went back to work. He changed his job the same day the artifact was completed, as if trying to distance himself from whatever he'd just done.

I have catalogued Ottanesmul. It is the most valuable object in the fortress. It is a throne with a picture of a bird that shouldn't exist, made from materials that shouldn't be here, by a dwarf who won't talk about it.

The children are definitely not allowed near this one either.

## The Second Kill — Sibrek Hamecrowded and the Goblin With No Obituary

*Autumn, Year 102.*

The second goblin came in autumn. **Stosbub Stalkerghouls** — a female goblin of the **Slitted Scourge**, born in Year -71. One hundred and seventy-three years old. Even older than the first one Zutthan killed last year.

Sibrek Hamecrowded was not supposed to be a warrior.

Sibrek is a **Talented Woodcrafter** — very rusty now, because militia commander Unib Keskalseng (a different Unib — this fortress has too many Unibs) saw "potential" in him and drafted him into the Brains of Crafting. Sibrek is an adequate organizer, novice at planting and herbalism, and his greatest joys are *intellectual discussions* and *watching performances*. He was drafted because someone thought a thoughtful woodcrafter who loves poetry would make a good soldier.

And then a goblin broke into the fortress, and Sibrek was the one standing between it and the rest of us.

The records show: **weapon item -1, shooter item -1**. No weapon. No bow.

Sibrek Hamecrowded — intellectual, poet, reluctant soldier — killed Stosbub Stalkerghouls, a one-hundred-and-seventy-three-year-old goblin assassin, with his **bare hands**. He bled her out. The cause of death reads simply: *blood*.

I wasn't there. I heard about it in the dining hall. Sibrek was sitting alone, staring at his food, and someone asked him what happened.

He said, "She came in. I was there. I didn't have a weapon."

"So what did you do?"

Long pause.

"I managed."

Sibrek is now a member of the **Company of Music** — our craftsdwarf guild. He doesn't talk about the kill. He goes to performances. He has intellectual discussions with his acquaintances. He secretly longs for peace so he can return to his true passion: thinking about things and talking about them.

He will not get peace. He is too useful. The militia commander will not release a dwarf who can kill goblins with his hands.

Two goblins of the Slitted Scourge — the destroyers of Bookbalded — dead on the floors of Luregold. If there is justice in this world, it was served by a father and a poet.

## The Incident With the Elves, as Witnessed by the Bookkeeper

*I was there. I wish I wasn't.*

The third caravan to visit Luregold was from **The Great Tresses** — an elven civilization involved in *seven wars*. Seven. They fight everyone. And yet, somehow, they still find time to send trading caravans to frontier dwarf fortresses in the middle of nowhere. I suppose commerce endures even when civilization doesn't.

Unib Townveiled, our Mayor and Broker — ninety-six years old, Proficient Appraiser — was assigned to handle the negotiation. I was nearby, updating the stockpile ledger. I should have intervened. I did not. This is my greatest failure as Bookkeeper.

Unib surveyed the trade goods. Our stockpiles were... specific. Ushrir had been producing masterpiece bone crafts at a rate that defied comprehension. Ingish, our Manager, had been approving work orders for shell crafts by the dozen because our fisherdwarves had generated a *mountain* of mussel shells. Bomrek had been adding his own masterworks to the heap.

The result: hundreds upon hundreds of shell crafts. Shell earrings. Shell bracelets. Shell figurines. Shell amulets. The stockpile room looked like a beach after a hurricane.

Unib, who is ninety-six years old and has learned a thing or two about appraisal, looked at the shell crafts, looked at the elves, and — I believe — made a calculated decision. She is a Proficient Appraiser. She knows what things are worth. She knows what she has.

What she did not know — or did not care about — is that elves consider animal products a moral abomination.

She pushed the shell crafts across the counter. *All of them.* Scores of them. A tide of carved mussel shell washing toward the elven merchants like the ocean reclaiming its dead.

The elves stared at the shells.
The elves stared at Unib.
The elves stared at each other.

I stared at my ledger and pretended to be very busy.

The elves packed their goods. They turned their wagons around in the three-wide corridor — the one we smoothed *specifically for caravans* — and the wagon wheels scraped the walls, and I heard the stone cry out. They left. Without a word. Without a trade. Without looking back.

Unib watched them leave. She took the wine she'd been planning to offer as a diplomatic sweetener, uncorked it, and drank it.

"More for us," she said.

I wrote in the ledger: *"Diplomatic incident — elves — UNSOLVED."* Then: *"Budget for militia equipment — URGENT."* Then I had a drink with Unib, because someone had to, and I am the only dwarf in this fortress who will sit with a ninety-six-year-old mayor who just offended an entire civilization and say, "That was spectacular."

She said, "They'll be back."

I said, "Will they?"

She drank.

"No," she said. "Probably not."

## On the Matter of Kobolds, Continuing

The filthy little creatures continue. Two more incursions this year — thefts, abduction attempts, the usual skulking. Chased off each time. Nobody died. Not even a kobold, which I find mildly disappointing.

Unib wants cage traps. I want a moat. We argued about this for an hour last Tuesday and resolved nothing, which is how most of our conversations end — with no conclusion and less wine than we started with. The cage traps would be cheaper. The moat would be more satisfying. Neither has been built. The kobolds continue to creep. I continue to complain. This is how governance works.

## On the Matter of the Guilds and the Faith

Three institutions now anchor the life of Luregold beyond mere survival: two guilds and one religion. I will note them for the ledger, because institutions outlast dwarves, and if we are to be remembered, it will be by what we built together rather than what we built alone.

**The Guild of Leopards** — our farmers' guild, established this year. *My* guild. I petitioned for it. I campaigned for it. When the guildhall was approved, I felt the deepest satisfaction I have ever recorded in this ledger. Not the farming — the *bureaucratic triumph*. I am a dwarf of documents and plump helmet wine, and I am not ashamed. Members include Lor Whiskeredboulder (who, you may recall, produced the artifact hatch cover while possessed last year), Tekkud Atticspark, Thikut Siegegilds, and others who understand that agriculture is a craft, not a chore.

Solon Faintdagger is not a member. He mines. He does not farm. He supports the guild from a distance, by which I mean he grunts approvingly when I mention it.

**The Company of Music** — our craftsdwarves' guild, also established this year. Founded by Ingish Blockadeconfused — co-founder of the fortress, Manager, Legendary Bone Carver. Zefon still carves and watches Ingish from across the workshop, and everyone still pretends not to notice. Members include Sibrek Hamecrowded (the bare-handed goblin killer), Ushrir Beardedspears (about whom I will have much more to say shortly), Bomrek Bandedinks, and more.

It says something about Luregold that our *warriors* are members of the *craftsdwarves' guild*, and our *craftsdwarves* occasionally kill goblins with their hands. We are not a military fortress. We are artisans who happen to be very, very good at violence when cornered.

**The Doctrine of Gold** — twenty-four worshippers pray to **Alron** in the **Sanctum of Silver**. The Holy Rock — the priest — is Ushrir Beardedspears, who also happens to be the most prolific crafter in the fortress. She performs the rites with wonder. The Sanctum is her domain, and for a few moments the world outside — with its wars and demons and Forgotten Beasts — doesn't exist.

Then someone knocks over a mug and the moment passes, and we go back to work.

## A Reckoning of Masterworks — The Crafter-Saints of Luregold

I have delayed this accounting long enough. The numbers demand it. The ledger demands it. And I will confess: I am proud of these numbers in a way that I am not proud of much else.

Eight hundred and sixty-six masterpieces line the halls of Luregold. Eight hundred and sixty-six objects of extraordinary quality, produced in less than three years by a fortress that started with seven dwarves and a pick. I have catalogued every one of them. My hand cramps. My ink runs low. I do not care. The numbers are *beautiful*.

And most of them were made by three dwarves.

**Ushrir Beardedspears** — four hundred and thirteen masterpieces. I need to talk about Ushrir.

She arrived in Wave Three, autumn of Year 100. She is a Legendary Bone Carver. She is the Holy Rock — the priest of the Doctrine of Gold. She is a member of the Company of Music. She has produced four hundred and thirteen masterpieces. Nearly half of everything beautiful in this fortress came from her hands.

She carves bone the way water runs downhill — inevitably, endlessly, without apparent effort. The rest of us produce a masterwork and feel *satisfied*. Ushrir produces a masterwork and moves on to the next bone. She mastered the craft in our first winter and has been operating at a level beyond mastery ever since. She doesn't just carve. She *prays*. In the summer of Year 101, she established a temple for the Doctrine of Gold and felt *wonder*. Wonder! A Legendary crafter, a priest, and she still feels *wonder* at the act of worship.

Her most recent thoughts: euphoria due to inebriation, and annoyance at having to drink without a goblet. There are plenty of goblets in this fortress. Ushrir owns at least three — I've catalogued them. The most likely explanation is that she got so profoundly drunk that she forgot where they were and drank directly from the barrel.

I asked her about it the next morning. She looked at me with the serene expression of a woman who has carved four hundred masterpieces and spoken to a god and said, "The barrel was closer."

The Doctrine of Gold has twenty-four worshippers. They deserve their saint.

**Bomrek Bandedinks** — two hundred and twenty-seven masterpieces. The second most prolific crafter in the fortress. Arrived Wave Five, Year 101. Between him and Ushrir, they account for six hundred and forty of our eight hundred and sixty-six masterworks. Three-quarters of everything beautiful in Luregold was made by two dwarves. I find this statistic either inspiring or terrifying, depending on how much wine I've had.

**Rovod Cobaltfortunes** — one hundred and forty-one masterpieces in less than a year. I've already mentioned her, but the number bears repeating. One hundred and forty-one. She is gaining on Bomrek. If she keeps this pace, she'll pass him before next winter. I suspect she doesn't care about the competition. I suspect she doesn't know there *is* a competition. She just works.

Between these three: seven hundred and eighty-one of eight hundred and sixty-six. The rest of us — Ingish with her fifty-nine, Zefon with his two fine carvings, all the other hands in all the other workshops — contribute the remainder. We are a fortress of many crafters and three miracles.

## The Mayor and the Bookkeeper — A Necessary Portrait

I have put this off because writing about oneself and one's... associates... requires a kind of honesty that is easier to avoid. But the ledger is for truth, and here is a truth:

Mayor **Unib Townveiled** is ninety-six years old. She has been alive since Year 6. She has a large family, worships several deities, has served as Broker and Mayor since Winter 101, and is classified in the records as a "passing acquaintance" of virtually every dwarf in the fortress.

Ninety-six years old and no close friends.

The records say she's too busy keeping order — mandates to enforce, petitions to hear, caravans to negotiate (or drive away, as the case may be). She knows everyone's name and nobody's secrets. She is a Talented Bone Carver, a Proficient Appraiser, and she was *annoyed* in our first summer because she had to drink without a goblet. She's been elected Mayor, watched performances, taken baths, and remembered every mandate deadline. She is competent at everything and intimate with nothing.

I am listed as her "passing acquaintance." This is technically accurate. We pass each other in the dining hall most evenings, and I acquaint her with whatever I'm drinking. She acquaints me with whatever complaint she has about the fish stockpile. We are not friends. We are something more efficient than friends.

Her most recent thought: euphoric due to inebriation.
My most recent thought: euphoric due to inebriation.

We understand each other.

## What the Autumn Caravan Told Me — The World Beyond Our Walls

The following intelligence comes from merchant gossip, migrant reports, and the occasional traumatized refugee who stumbles into Luregold muttering about fire and death. I have sorted it by how much I believe it, which is to say: not very much, but enough to lose sleep over.

**The Riddled Sins have gone mad.** Every caravan brings the same story. The goblin civilization of the Riddled Sins — led by the demon Mothida Routveiled — has fractured into warring pieces. They are fighting *everyone*. They are fighting *themselves*. The merchants speak of a civil war among the goblins — a score of battles, factions turning on factions. A vast conflict against the elves of the Golden-Butterfly of Lobsters that has raged across scores of engagements — the largest war in the world, by the merchants' telling. Further wars against other elves, against rival goblin civilizations. The Riddled Sins were always the most dangerous goblin power — a multiracial horde of goblins, corrupted elves, and turned dwarves. Now they've shattered. The continent shakes with it. Everyone has heard.

I note this with cautious relief. Goblins fighting goblins is good for us. Goblins *finishing* their civil war and looking outward for new enemies is very bad for us.

**The Everseeing Deceiver has taken a dwarven fortress.** This news arrived with the autumn migrants, who could barely speak the words. The goblin civilization of the Everseeing Deceiver — ruled by the demon Urem — conquered **Valleypick**, a fortress of our kin in the Lancer of Rumors, in Year 102. Some twenty battles in the Conflict of Scabs preceded the fall.

A dwarven fortress in goblin hands. I have written this fact in the ledger and underlined it three times. If it can happen to the Lancer of Rumors, it can happen to us.

**Our sister civilization is dying.** The Lancer of Rumors — dwarves like us, kin of our kin — have lost nearly half their people. Of some seven hundred historical souls, roughly three hundred and fifty remain breathing. They are fighting both the Everseeing Deceiver goblins *and* the elves of the Golden-Butterfly of Lobsters. They've lost Valleypick. They are surrounded.

If anyone from the Lancer of Rumors arrives at Luregold seeking refuge, we should take them in. And then check them for bite marks, because with that many dead, there are probably vampires.

**The demon Dungboil is still out there.** Migrants who passed through Bookbalded's ruins — from a safe distance — report that the demon Dungboil the Gall of Filths was present during the massacre of Year 94. It has killed more of our people than any living creature — the merchants say roughly a hundred and forty, though the exact count depends on who's telling the story and how drunk they are. It is unaligned with any goblin civilization. It goes where it pleases. It kills what it finds.

If Dungboil comes to Luregold, the Bookkeeper's recommendation is: close the drawbridge, seal the gates, and pray to whatever gods still listen. If there is no drawbridge, *build one*.

The merchants whisper other names when the wine flows and the lanterns burn low. A bronze colossus called **Athis Thundereddent** that has killed over a hundred souls and dwells in a lair somewhere to the east — the traders can't agree where, but they agree it's large and angry and made of metal. A titan called **Inefa Passdeer** with nearly as many kills, lurking in another lair. And then there is the Roc — **Thiken Prestigesaviors the Waxes of Weather** — and this one I take personally, because the Roc has killed *our* people. Kubuk Floorfountains in Year 91. Degel Siegeceilings in Year 94. Clinching dwarves, specifically targeted, specifically dead. The Roc has over sixty kills to its name and it is still hunting.

A dragon called Cobim Parchedtaxed roams somewhere — the caravans disagree on where. A hydra named Enshal Sweatbegins, with several dozen kills, wanders the wilds. Any of them could arrive at Luregold with no warning, no reason, and no mercy. They are forces of nature. One does not negotiate with a bronze colossus.

**The Forgotten Beasts have not been touched.** I write this with red ink because it deserves red ink. The deep miners speak of hundreds of them — great ancient things dwelling in the cavern layers, waiting. Every single one is alive. In over a hundred years of recorded history, not a single civilization has killed a single Forgotten Beast. I find this statistic so alarming that I've now written it twice — once in the ledger and once here.

One of them — Ngedlu Veilspurt the Frothy — already surfaced at Bookbalded in Year 92 and killed two of our people before retreating into the dark. It's still down there. They're *all* still down there.

Some choice names, as reported by deep miners and madmen: Sedme the Night of Caverns, whose sphere is *murder*. Ur the Seizure of Mange, whose sphere is *nightmares*. Something called the Crab of Shadows, associated with deformity. Vafice Divebelch the Hateful Puke — and yes, that is really what the miners call it — whose sphere is *depravity*. The Punch-Inferno of Fights, whose sphere is *chaos*.

*(Marginal note, underlined twice: "Do not dig below the third cavern layer. Do not. DO NOT." I know no one will listen. I am writing it anyway.)*

**The necromancers.** The tower of the Molten Glazes — once some fifty strong — has been whittled down to a handful of living practitioners. Their war against the Strategic Confederations grinds on. A second tower, the Mythical Touches, sits quiet with a dozen members. Neither is near us. For now.

**Distant elf politics.** Various elven factions are warring with various goblin factions across the continent. Unless the elves we *personally offended* decide to add us to their list — and Unib assures me they won't, though she said this while drinking the wine she'd meant for them — this is someone else's problem.

## The Searing Conflict — An Update for Year 102

Our war with the Tall Empires continues, though "war" implies a conflict between equals, and there is nothing equal about this anymore.

The elves of the Lilac of Perfecting have conquered both **Latheredvaults** and **Rainymule** from the Tall Empires this year. Scores of battles between the elves and the humans, and the humans lose every one. The Tall Empires — once over two hundred souls — are down to roughly ninety humans. They picked fights with everyone: us, the goblins, a breakaway human faction, and the elves. The elves are dismembering them from the west. The goblins gnaw from the east. We hold the line in the center and watch.

This war will end when the Tall Empires cease to exist. Which may be soon.

Luregold has not been attacked. Our settlements at Pantrags, Diamondgates, and Sculpturecloister have taken casualties in prior years, but our gates remain unbreached. The Bookkeeper notes this with gratitude and paranoia in equal measure.

## The Bookkeeper's Assessment of Threats Both Near and Deep

I, Atir Dumatturel, having reviewed all available intelligence and consumed a responsible quantity of plump helmet wine, present the following assessment for the consideration of the Mayor, the militia commander, and anyone else who happens to read the margins of the fortress ledger.

**On the matters that keep the Bookkeeper awake at night:** We are at war. The Searing Conflict with the Tall Empires has already cost Clinching lives at Pantrags, Diamondgates, and Sculpturecloister — ten dwarves dead across those sites in two years. Luregold has not been hit, but I refuse to call this safety. I call it *delay*. Furthermore, two goblin infiltrators of the Slitted Scourge have already breached our walls. Two came. Two died. More are coming. The Bookkeeper recommends: arm the militia with actual metal weapons, not the wooden training gear they've been carrying like children with sticks.

**On the matters that should concern any dwarf with sense:** The megabeasts are real and they are hungry. The Roc Thiken has killed our people by name. The demon Dungboil has killed more Clinching dwarves than most goblin warbands. Any of the great monsters could arrive at Luregold with zero warning. The Bookkeeper recommends a drawbridge and the engineering knowledge to close it in a hurry. Additionally, the fall of Valleypick proves that goblin civilizations with demons at their head can and will conquer dwarven fortresses. If the Lancer of Rumors collapses entirely, their conquerors will look for new targets. And then there are the elves we offended. Unib doesn't think this is a problem. The Bookkeeper disagrees. The Bookkeeper has noted his disagreement in the ledger and moves on.

**On the matters that are annoying but not yet lethal:** Kobolds. Four incursions across two years, increasing boldness. Not lethal yet. Merely *aggravating*. Also, the Riddled Sins goblin civil war — beneficial to us while it lasts, catastrophic when it ends and the victors start looking outward.

**On the matters that are distant but inevitable:** The caverns. The deep miners speak of hundreds of Forgotten Beasts, and not one has been killed in over a century of recorded history. Luregold has not breached the deep cavern layers. When it does — *when*, not if, because our miners are dwarves and dwarves dig — the Bookkeeper would like it noted that he recommended against it in writing. In triplicate. With a marginal drawing of a skull. The Bookkeeper recommends the drawing be taken seriously.

## The Ledger Entry for Year 102, Month 12

*Written by lamplight. Plump helmet wine within reach. The fortress is quiet — or as quiet as eighty-eight dwarves ever get, which is to say: someone is always hammering.*

Luregold stands. Three winters endured. Eighty-eight dwarves, all breathing. Five artifacts forged by hands guided by forces I do not understand — a hatch cover, mussel shell earrings, a stone box, a blowgun, and a throne with a giant loon on it. Eight hundred and sixty-six masterworks lining the halls, nearly half of them by one woman who also happens to be our priest. Two goblins of the Slitted Scourge, dead. Zero dwarves lost.

We are the youngest fortress of the Guilds of Clinching, and — for the moment — the luckiest. Bookbalded reminds me what happens when luck runs out. One hundred and fifty dead in a single year. The Tired Spears, extinguished. The Stirred Shield, broken.

But we are not Bookbalded. Not yet.

Our walls are smoothed. Our corridors are wide enough for caravans — even elven caravans, should they ever deign to return after what Unib did to them. Our craftsdwarves produce masterworks at a pace that borders on mania. Our defenders have killed everything that's come through the door — one with a weapon and one with his bare hands. Our priest prays in the Sanctum of Silver and the faithful gather and for a moment the world is not ending.

Solon mines. He doesn't talk about it. He doesn't need to. Ingish manages. Zefon carves and watches her from across the workshop and thinks nobody notices. I notice. I notice everything. It's my job.

The Mayor is in the dining hall. I can hear her from here. She is drinking without a goblet again and will be annoyed about it tomorrow. I will be there tomorrow too. We will sit across from each other and she will complain about the fish stockpile and I will complain about the ledger and neither of us will say that we are glad the other one is still alive, because dwarves don't say things like that. But we will both be thinking it.

The wars will continue. The goblins will come. The kobolds will creep. The elves will remember. The Riddled Sins will fight themselves. The Tall Empires will die.

And somewhere, deep below the lowest stairway Solon has carved, in the black places where the water drips and the fungus glows and the stone groans under the weight of the world above it — something enormous shifts in its sleep.

It knows we're here.
