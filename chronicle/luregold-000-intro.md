# The Chronicles of Luregold

## *Being the Personal Account of Atir Dumatturel, Bookkeeper, Grandmaster Planter, and Founding Dwarf of Luregold*

---

*Written by my hand — Atir Roughlearning, son of nobody important, Grandmaster Planter, Adept Record Keeper (rusty, I know, shut up), co-founder of the Guild of Leopards (our farmers' guild, and I will fight anyone who says planting isn't a craft), and one of the seven idiots who thought carving a hole in the ground in the middle of a world war was a good idea.*

*It was a great idea.*

*The ink stains on this page are ink. The other stains are plump helmet wine. I will not be taking questions.*

*My drinking companion, Mayor Unib Townveiled — who is also the Trade Broker, because of course she is, because this fortress gives every important job to the same three people — may corroborate some of these accounts. She is ninety-six years old, knows everyone, is close friends with nobody, and is currently euphoric due to inebriation. We have that in common.*

---

## On the Matter of Our People

I should begin with who we are, since any fool who finds this manuscript in the rubble — and I hope to every god in the pantheon that no one ever does — will need to understand the world before they understand our little hole in the ground.

We are dwarves of **The Guilds of Clinching**, a civilization as old as this world. Our capital, **Craftpractice**, was carved in Year 1 — the very first year the world drew breath — and we have been carving ever since. The old records, such as they are, claim we hold some thirty-odd sites: fortresses, hillocks, mountain halls, scattered across the land like stubborn teeth in a jaw that's taken too many punches.

As for our numbers — I have done my best to compile a reckoning from ledger fragments, migrant reports, and the kind of oral tradition that gets hazier with every retelling. By my estimate, some seven hundred dwarves draw breath under the banner of Clinching. We have buried over three hundred. Nearly half of those dead fell at a single fortress, in a single year. I will come to that presently. More wine first.

We are not the only dwarven civilization. Our kin in **The Lancer of Rumors** cling to their holdings to the east, though "cling" is generous — the last reports suggest they have lost nearly as many souls as they have kept. And there are others further afield, names carried on merchant tongues that I cannot verify. But the Guilds of Clinching are *my* people, and this is *my* ledger, and so they are the people you will hear about.

## The Seven Who Struck Earth

In the spring of Year 100, seven dwarves walked into the wilderness. The world was on fire — the wars had just erupted, Bookbalded's ashes were still warm, and someone at Craftpractice decided that *now* was the time to send seven people to start a new fortress.

Those seven people were us. I was there. I remember all of it. Mostly.

**Solon Faintdagger** — Legendary Miner. The quiet one. Our expedition leader before Unib took over as mayor. Solon doesn't talk much, but when he does, it's worth listening to. He worships Tunur Gaisseng Lemloridor — the only one of us who doesn't follow Alron or Thusest. I asked him about it once. He said, "Different gods, same stone." Then he went back to mining.

He's close friends with Ingish and a kindred spirit with Zefon — the three of them have something easy between them, the kind of bond you get when you've carved the same stairway in the dark at three in the morning while something howled in the distance. I respect Solon Faintdagger more than anyone in this fortress, and I will *never* tell him that.

**Myself, Atir Roughlearning** — Grandmaster Planter, Adept Record Keeper, chronic arguer, and apparently the only person willing to write things down. I keep the books. I keep the farm plots. I keep getting into arguments. I am a dwarf of documents and plump helmet wine, and I am not ashamed.

> *The Bookkeeper notes, for the record, that his most recent thought is euphoria due to inebriation. As is tradition.*

**Shorast Channeledplans** — One of the original seven. Steady. Reliable. Worships Dishmab Stonesaved. Doesn't cause problems. Doesn't solve them either. Every fortress needs a Shorast — a dwarf who shows up, does the work, and never once makes you write an incident report. If civilizations were built on heroes alone, they'd have collapsed centuries ago. They endure because of dwarves like Shorast, who carry stones and ask no questions.

**Moldath Pickrecluses** — Another founder. Worships Alron, same as Ingish. I don't have much to say about Moldath, which is itself a compliment in a fortress where "nothing to report" means "nobody died today." Moldath is the kind of dwarf who makes "nothing to report" possible.

**Dastot Searchlashed** — The youngest of the original seven, born in Year 80 — only twenty when we set out. A woman barely out of adolescence who volunteered for an expedition into the unknown while wars raged across the continent. She worships Tunur Satinring, same as Solon. She came here with faith and stubbornness and not much else. She's still here. I admire that more than I will ever say to her face.

**Zefon Furpicks** — Adept Stone Carver, Skilled Stonecutter, our fortress's stone artist. He worships Thusest. He wanders with this faraway look, stepping around piles of fish corpses left by our overenthusiastic fisherdwarves, and he never complains about it. He just... carves. Quietly. Contentedly.

If you asked him his happiest moments, he'd list sleeping in a good bedroom, dining in a very good dining room, and watching performances. Simple pleasures. He is a simple dwarf. He has produced masterpieces — stone carvings that make the dining hall worth dining in — and treated each one as though it were merely the natural consequence of sitting down and paying attention to the rock.

He is also deeply, helplessly in love with Ingish Blockadeconfused, and everyone in the fortress knows it. I could see it from the first week, when he kept finding reasons to be wherever she was hauling stone. I suspect the stone stockpile noticed before he did.

**Ingish Blockadeconfused** — Legendary Bone Carver, Talented Organizer, and the actual reason this fortress functions. She would become the **Manager of Luregold** and co-founder of the **Company of Music**, our craftsdwarf guild — but I am getting ahead of myself. Even in those first days, before the titles and the work orders and the guildhall petition she'd later file with such *jubilation*, Ingish was the one keeping us organized. She wears the pants in her relationship with Zefon, and she wears the pants in this fortress. Always has.

Dozens of masterpieces to her name already — bone carvings of a quality that makes the rest of us feel like we're playing with mud. She approves the work orders. She runs the ledger of production. She is, at any given moment, simultaneously satisfied at work and mildly exasperated with everyone around her.

Their romance — Zefon's and Ingish's — would begin in our first spring at Luregold, and I will have more to say about it when I begin the annual reviews. For now, let the record show that by the time the seven of us struck earth, the glances had already started. Zefon made eyes. Ingish pretended not to notice. The rest of us pretended not to notice either.

We were all very bad at pretending.

## The Scars of Clinching

Before I write of Luregold — before I set down the annual reviews of what we built and who arrived and which goblins had the poor judgment to test our walls — I must account for the wounds. A fortress does not exist in silence. We were born from a civilization that was *bleeding*, and if you do not understand the blood, you will not understand why seven dwarves marched into the wild in the spring of Year 100 with war on every horizon and hope in only one direction: down.

What follows is compiled from old ledger fragments, migrant testimony, caravan gossip, and the kind of oral tradition that smooths the edges of horror into something a dwarf can repeat over dinner without losing his appetite. I have done my best with the numbers. The further from Luregold, the less certain I am. That is the honest truth of record-keeping: precision is a luxury of proximity.

### The Fall of Notchedgalley (Year 69)

The first great wound. The fortress of **Notchedgalley** — founded in Year 61, barely eight years old — fell to forces the records do not name clearly. Forty-odd dwarves died within its halls. Whether the attackers were goblins, beasts, or something worse, no reliable account survives. Either nobody lived to report, or the Bookkeeper at the time was even worse at his job than the current one.

Notchedgalley's site government, **The Stirred Shield**, was extinguished. Zero members remain. The name persists only in ledgers that no one reads, and now in this manuscript, because I believe the dead deserve at least that much.

### The Forgotten Beast Beneath Bookbalded (Year 92)

Before the goblins came, the deep places struck first. The Forgotten Beast **Ngedlu Veilspurt the Frothy** — a creature of nightmares and caverns, the sort of thing the old miners speak of in low voices after too much ale — surfaced within Bookbalded in Year 92 and killed two dwarves: **Kulet Walldawn** and **Bomrek Roombreaths**.

Their names are recorded. The beast retreated. It was not killed.

> *They never are.*

### What We Lost at Bookbalded (Year 94)

*This is the wound that still bleeds.*

In Year 94, the goblin warband called **The Slitted Scourge** descended upon Bookbalded. What followed was not a siege. It was an extermination. A hundred and fifty dwarves died in a single year, or near enough — the tallies from Bookbalded are incomplete, and I have rounded where the ink has faded. The battle the survivors named "The Assaults of Murdering" was, by every account, the second-largest single engagement in our world's recorded history.

And the demon came with them.

**Dungboil the Gall of Filths** — a creature of the deepest pits, a demon unaligned with any goblin civilization — was present at Bookbalded. It killed our people. **Dumat Ruleranvil** fell to Dungboil's hand. Others followed. The merchants say Dungboil has taken more lives than any single creature walking the surface, and I have no cause to doubt them.

The Roc **Thiken Prestigesaviors the Waxes of Weather** — a megabeast the caravans speak of with the particular dread reserved for things that hunt from the sky — also struck at Clinching holdings around this time. It killed **Degel Siegeceilings** at an unknown location. The Roc has tasted dwarf before. It remembers the flavor.

Bookbalded fell. Its site government, **The Tired Spears**, was destroyed. Zero members remain. The fortress itself still stands — goblins occupy it now, squatting in halls our people carved — and from what the migrants say, skirmishes continue within those walls even today. The goblins hold Bookbalded, but they hold it *uneasily*.

I note, with the grim precision that is my only professional virtue, that Bookbalded accounts for nearly half of all the dwarves our civilization has ever buried. Half of everyone we have lost, in one fortress, in one year, in one terrible autumn.

> *I have written this fact in the ledger and underlined it three times. If you think I am being dramatic, you did not know anyone at Bookbalded. I did not know them either. But I have counted them, and counting the dead is its own form of mourning.*

### The Savage Attack at Cavechew (Year 98)

Four years after Bookbalded, the goblins tested us again. A massive force — the **Unbridled Poison** and their allies — assaulted the fortress of **Cavechew**. What the survivors call "The Savage Attack" was the single largest battle in recorded history, if you reckon by the count of individual engagements — nearly four hundred clashes in a single sprawling melee.

Twenty-nine Clinching dwarves died. The goblin commanders — Zolak Slimblack, Ngoso Dungeoncontains, Stozu Dungeonbolt, and a troll warlord whose name I will not dignify by remembering correctly — all participated in the assault.

But Cavechew *held*. The fortress endured. The walls did not break. The Guilds of Clinching do not break easily.

### The Wars That Made Us

Our civilization has fought three wars in total, as the old records reckon it:

- **The Crazy War** (years 94 to 95) — The Slitted Scourge attacked us. Three battles. Bookbalded fell. A hundred and fifty dead. Our darkest hour, and the reason half the migrants who arrive at Luregold flinch when they hear the word "goblin."

- **The Conflict of Battle** (year 98) — The Unbridled Poison came for Cavechew. One great battle. Twenty-nine dead. We held.

- **The Searing Conflict** (year 100 to present) — **The Tall Empires**, a human civilization, declared war upon us in the very year Luregold was founded. I will have more to say about this war when I begin the annual reviews, for it is *our* war — the one that burns while we build. For now, let the record note: it is ongoing, the humans are losing, and we are not yet losing. That is the best I can offer.

> *The Bookkeeper recommends more wine before proceeding to the annual entries. The history is finished. The present is harder.*

## Into the Wilderness

Spring, Year 100. The Crazy War two years behind us. The Conflict of Battle two years behind us. The Searing Conflict not yet declared, though I suspect the diplomats were already sharpening their grievances in distant halls.

Seven dwarves left Craftpractice. We carried picks, an anvil, and the kind of desperate optimism that only makes sense if you've spent your whole life watching other fortresses fall and thinking, *not mine. Not this time.*

Solon led. He didn't say much on the march — he never does — but he chose the site: a hillside with good stone, clean water, and enough distance from the nearest goblin lair that we might have time to dig before they noticed us. Ingish kept the supply list. Zefon walked beside her and tried to look like he wasn't walking beside her. Shorast and Moldath hauled. Dastot, twenty years old and lighter than her pack, kept pace without complaint. I brought up the rear, because someone had to watch for trouble, and because I was already composing the first entry in this ledger in my head.

The trees thinned. The stone showed through the soil. Solon stopped, looked at the ground, looked at the sky, and drove his pick into the earth.

Nobody said anything. We didn't need to.

We were home.

*What followed — the digging, the building, the migrants, the romances, the goblins, the artifacts, the wars that reached even our doorstep — I will record in the annual reviews that follow this preamble. One year at a time. One entry at a time. The wine will hold.*

---

*— Atir Dumatturel, called Roughlearning, Bookkeeper of Luregold, Grandmaster Planter, Co-Founder of the Guild of Leopards, and one of the seven who struck earth in the spring of Year 100.*

*If this chronicle is found beside my skeleton, the wine cellar is three levels down and to the left. Help yourself. I certainly did.*
